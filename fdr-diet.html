The FDR Diet: From Dirty Martinis to 7-Cent Lunches

Franklin D. Roosevelt is remembered for leading the U.S. through the Great Depression and WWII, but inside the White House, his kitchen was a battlefield of its own. Between his personal aristocratic tastes and Eleanor Roosevelt’s "scientific" frugal menus, the meals served to the 32nd President were anything but boring.

What was on the Menu?

Based on our recent breakdown, here are the key staples that fueled the Roosevelt presidency:

  • The Dirty Martini 2 parts Gin, 1 part Dry Vermouth, and a splash of olive brine. FDR was a "lousy bartender" who loved to shake his martinis—a major social faux pas at the time!
  • Scrambled Eggs A Sunday night tradition. Eleanor Roosevelt would cook them herself in a chafing dish right at the table.
  • Green Peppers Stuffed with Rice One of the few "budget" meals FDR actually enjoyed during the Depression era.
  • Terrapin (Turtle) Soup A luxury of the era. FDR loved Maryland-style turtle soup, often served with a side of "Fairy Toast."
  • Thick Steak with Butter When he could escape Eleanor's "7.5 cent lunches," he indulged in heavy, buttered steaks.

The Food FDR "Missed"

While the video covers the highlights, Roosevelt’s diet had some even stranger quirks. Did you know he famously served Hot Dogs to King George VI and Queen Elizabeth? The move horrified his mother but delighted the American public, proving that even a king can enjoy a 5-cent frankfurter with mustard.

He also had a deep love for grilled cheese sandwiches and Lake Superior whitefish, though his housekeeper, Henrietta Nesbitt, was infamous for serving him "insipid" and bland versions of his favorite foods to save money.
